🌱 Mapping and Classifying Mangroves Using Remote Sensing and AI

Mangroves are the unsung heroes of coastal ecosystems—protecting shorelines, supporting biodiversity, and acting as carbon sinks. In this post, I’ll walk you through how I mapped mangrove vegetation in Maharashtra using satellite imagery, machine learning, and deep learning.

📍 Study Area

My study focused on the coastal districts of Maharashtra, India, which together contribute 6.5% of the country’s mangrove cover.

🛰️ Satellite Data & Tools

  • Landsat 8 OLI data (bands 4, 5, and 6)
  • ArcGIS Pro for radiometric correction & clipping
  • Google Colab + Python for ML/DL model training

🔄 Image Preprocessing

After applying radiometric correction, I used spectral indices and clipped the data to the study region. Then, images were divided into 256x256 tiles and labeled as mangrove or non-mangrove.

🤖 Model Building

Two models were used for classification:

  • Random Forest – Traditional ML using Scikit-Learn
  • Convolutional Neural Network (CNN) – Deep learning model built with Keras
