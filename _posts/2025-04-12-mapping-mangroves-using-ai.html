üåø Mapping the Mangroves: A Tech-Driven Approach to Preserving Coastal Ecosystems

Mangroves‚Äîthose tangled, salt-tolerant forests along tropical coasts‚Äîare more than just picturesque landscapes. They are powerful carbon sinks, natural disaster shields, and nurseries for marine life. Yet, despite their ecological and economic value, mangroves are rapidly declining due to urban expansion, pollution, and climate change. To protect these vital ecosystems, accurate and efficient mapping techniques are essential. That‚Äôs where our study comes in.

üõ∞ Why Map Mangroves?

Mangrove forests are tough to access, often submerged and scattered across remote coastlines. Traditional field surveys are expensive and time-consuming. Our work leverages satellite imagery and machine learning to streamline this process, enabling large-scale, data-driven monitoring of mangrove health and distribution.

üß† The Heart of the Study: Remote Sensing Meets AI

We used Landsat 8 satellite images‚Äîfreely available and rich in spectral data‚Äîto detect mangrove coverage along the coastal districts of Maharashtra, India. Landsat's blend of visible, near-infrared, and shortwave infrared bands makes it ideal for distinguishing mangroves from other vegetation types.

To analyze this data, we turned to ArcGIS Pro for preprocessing and spatial analysis. But the real magic happened in Google Colab, where we trained Random Forest and Convolutional Neural Network (CNN) models to classify mangrove vs. non-mangrove regions.

üß™ Methodology Breakdown

  1. Radiometric Correction: Converted raw pixel values into surface reflectance using metadata-based formulas.
  2. Band Composites & Spectral Indices: Used specific Landsat bands (especially Band 4, 5, and 6) to enhance vegetation features.
  3. Image Tiling: Split the corrected images into 256x256-pixel tiles, labeled as mangrove or non-mangrove.
  4. Data Augmentation: Increased dataset diversity by rotating images, ensuring robust training.
  5. Model Training:
    • Random Forest (RF): Ensemble method using decision trees.
    • CNN: Deep learning model using multiple convolution and pooling layers to learn complex patterns in imagery.
