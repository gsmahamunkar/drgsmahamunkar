Mapping Mangroves Using Deep Learning: A Case Study on the Maharashtra Coast

Author: Dr. Geetanjali Mahamunkar

Date: April 12, 2025

Mangroves are vital coastal ecosystems that provide a range of ecosystem services—from carbon sequestration to coastal protection. This blog post discusses my thesis work, which utilizes deep learning techniques to automate and enhance the accuracy of mangrove mapping using satellite imagery along the Maharashtra coast of India.

Study Area: Maharashtra Coast

The focus area includes several coastal districts of Maharashtra—Raigad, Ratnagiri, Sindhudurg, Thane, Mumbai, and Palghar.

Data Collection and Preprocessing

  • Used Sentinel-2 Level-2A imagery from 2021
  • Ground truth polygons were created via Google Earth Pro and fieldwork validation
  • Image tiles were generated using a sliding window approach of size 128x128 pixels

Deep Learning Approach

We used a Convolutional Neural Network (CNN)-based model with layers fine-tuned to classify mangrove vs. non-mangrove patches.

Model Training and Validation

  • Training-Validation-Test split: 60%-20%-20%
  • Binary Cross-Entropy loss and Adam optimizer used
  • Data augmentation for robustness (rotations, flips)

Results

Model Accuracy Precision Recall F1-Score
Custom CNN 94.8% 93.5% 96.1% 94.8%

Conclusion

This study demonstrates the potential of using deep learning models like CNNs to support environmental monitoring efforts. The high accuracy of the model suggests that AI-driven approaches can complement traditional mangrove mapping techniques and be scaled across larger geographies.
